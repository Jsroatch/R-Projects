## Analytical Framework

All player evaluations use efficiency metrics from **BartTorvik** and **EvanMiya** — the same models used by NBA front offices to evaluate college talent. The key stats throughout this report:

| Stat | What It Means | Why It Matters for Self's System |
|---|---|---|
| **ORtg** | Points scored per 100 possessions | Higher = more efficient offense |
| **DRtg** | Points allowed per 100 while on floor | **Self's #1 non-negotiable** — if you can't guard, you won't play |
| **BPM** | Overall value added per 100 possessions | Best single-number measure of impact |
| **TS%** | True Shooting % (accounts for 3s and FTs) | Scoring efficiency |
| **ORB%** | % of available offensive rebounds grabbed | Core identity stat for Kansas bigs |
| **3P%** | Three-point percentage | Floor spacing in Hi-Lo sets |

**Bill Self's non-negotiable thresholds:**
- DRtg **≤ 102** for rotation players
- ORB% **> 6%** for bigs
- 3P% **> 34%** for perimeter rotation

## Recommended Portal Targets — Scenario A

### First Priority: Lead Guard

> **Devin Carter — PG, Providence** `$1,600,000`

| ORtg | DRtg | BPM | TS% | 3P% | Steal% |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 119 | 95 | +6.2 | 61.0% | 35.8% | 3.0% |

Carter is the ideal Bill Self point guard. His defensive rating of 95 is **elite** — among the best nationally for his position. He pushes pace in transition without sacrificing half-court structure, and his steal rate (3.0%) signals the activity and ball pressure Self demands. He's the rarest type of guard: one who makes your offense better *and* your defense better. At Providence he hasn't had the national stage his numbers deserve, which may hold his NIL ask below market for a guard of his caliber.

**Fit grade: A+** | **Value tier: Premium**

### Second Priority: Scoring Guard

> **Johnell Davis — SG, FAU** `$1,500,000` ⭐ *Top undervalued target*

| ORtg | DRtg | BPM | TS% | 3P% | 3P Rate | Usage |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 121 | 97 | +6.8 | 62.5% | 37.2% | 44% | 28% |

Davis is the standout analytical finding of this entire report. His offensive rating of **121 is top-20 nationally** among all guards, and he does it at a 28% usage rate with a 44% three-point attempt rate — he's not padding numbers in a bad system, he's genuinely elite. His defensive rating of 97 clears Self's threshold with room to spare.

The reason he's underpriced: he plays at FAU. Mid-major exposure discount means programs aren't bidding him up the way they would if he played at an SEC school with identical numbers. At $1.5M he is likely **$400–700K below what a Power 4 player with his metrics would command.** He fills the Darryn Peterson production void with better defensive upside.

**Fit grade: A+** | **Value tier: Undervalued (mid-major discount)**

# Methodology Notes

**Efficiency metrics** are derived from BartTorvik and EvanMiya frameworks, using
2024–25 season data as the primary baseline with development trajectory adjustments.

**NIL salary estimates** are inferred using a multiplier model anchored to publicly
reported 2024–25 portal deals (On3, 247Sports industry reporting). The model applies:
- Position scarcity (centers +25%, lead guards +15%)
- Program exposure factor (mid-major players receive a ~20% market discount vs. equivalent P4 players — creating the "undervalue opportunity" identified above)
- Defensive premium (Self's system specifically rewards defenders)
- Market growth (~18% from 2024–25 baseline to 2026–27)

**Confidence interval on NIL estimates: ±20–30%.** The market has high variance. These figures represent a reasonable negotiating range, not a fixed price.

**Portal availability** is a projection based on eligibility status and program context. All availability and salary estimates should be recalibrated once actual portal entries and 2025–26 season-end stats are available.
